An endangered species is one that is likely to become extinct because of a rapid decrease in population or loss of habitat. The decrease in population typically leads to a loss of genetic diversity. The end-result is inbreeding which leads to an increase in disease and fewer offspring.

The International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) keeps a "Red List" of threatened species that consists of seven levels:

  • extinct
  • extinct in the wild
  • critically endangered
  • endangered
  • vulnerable
  • near threatened
  • least concern
